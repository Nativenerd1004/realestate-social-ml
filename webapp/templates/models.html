SMOTE for Class Imbalance

The Host Churn target had only 2.85% positive class — severely skewed. Synthetic Minority Oversampling (SMOTE) was applied before training to balance classes, directly addressing the F1 plateau from the previous iteration that sat at 0.59.

3 Independent Models

Each prediction tab uses a separately trained model, scaler, and feature set. No shared weights — Host Churn, Social Media Performance, and Listing Sale are fully independent pipelines tailored to their dataset's structure.

Why RandomForest Dominates

Random Forest consistently outperforms across all 3 targets due to its ensemble averaging, robustness to feature scale (even after MinMaxScaler), and natural handling of mixed categorical + continuous features without needing further preprocessing.
